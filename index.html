Giving New Life To Old Stuff!

Recycling is more than just a trend, it's a responsibility we all share to protect our planet. By giving our waste a second life, we can reduce the amount of waste in landfills, conserve natural resources, and reduce pollution. From turning plastic bottles into new clothing to transforming cardboard into packaging materials, recycling is a small action that can make a big impact. So, let's all do our part and recycle, because a cleaner and healthier world is in our hands.

How you can help!

The state of our planet's environment is something that should concern us all. Every single day, we have the power to make a difference and take steps towards a cleaner, greener future. One of the biggest threats to our planet is plastic pollution and waste pollution. But you can be a part of the solution. It starts with small, everyday choices like saying no to single-use plastic items, recycling properly, and reducing your overall waste. You can also support businesses and organizations that prioritize sustainability and eco-friendly practices. By making these conscious choices, you are not only reducing your impact on the environment, but you are also inspiring others to follow in your footsteps. Together, we can create a world where plastic pollution and waste pollution are a thing of the past. Remember, even the smallest actions can have a big impact.

Recycling Location Highlight

Richard S. Burns, which began as a one-person local cleanout operation in the 1960s, has now become the only complete recycling and waste recovery facility in Delaware Valley. Their extensive 13-acre compound is capable of processing more than 1,500 tons of waste and materials each day. Throughout their more than four decades in the industry, Richard S. Burns has continued to grow and innovate to meet the evolving needs of their customers. They were pioneers of recycling and waste recovery long before the advent of green technology, and have since developed new techniques and products to cater to their expanding clientele.
